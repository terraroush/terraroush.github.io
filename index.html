My Coding Journey

The following projects are listed in descending order. I've been staying busy learning VueJS. Featured here are also my capstone projects from my time at Nashville Software School. I've included a group project and a couple very beginning projects to show the road traveled.

Memories Matching Game

I created this matching game using VueJS. This was an assignment meant to get us to practice iteration with v-for, using component data, component methods, CSS class binding, passing props, emitting events and using computed properties.

Test your memory here

Dune Trivia Game

I also made this trivia game with VueJS. This assignment, while highlighting my love of Dune, was practice in iteration with v-for, using component data, conditional rendering with v-if, simple methods, passing props and emiting events, and using computer properties.

Test your Dune knowledge here

Spelling Test

This spelling test is again, a practice assignment from my Vue course. This was practice with simple model binding with v-model, conditional rendering with v-if, component data, component methods, passing props and emiting events, and using computed properties.

Test your spelling savvy here

TheList

TheList is my back-end capstone from NSS made with C#/.NET with a ReactJS front-end. I created this app with busy households in mind. TheList solves the problem of one person having sole responsibility over groceries. TheList is meant to be interactive so that multiple users can affect one list to make sure everyone is represented.

client recall

client recall is my front-end capstone project from NSS made with ReactJS. I created this app for hairstylists, to solve the very real problem of client information storage. Most hair stylists I know, have used index cards their entire careers to save and update important data such as hair color formulas.

About Me

Over the last ten years, I took over 9000 appointments as a hairdresser/stylist/guru/artist. I worked alongside and with over 40 other uniquely talented individuals. I excelled in wedding hair, detailed and picture-perfect hair color transformations and even barbering. I was always learning, trying to challenge myself, and pushing to do my best work.

Amidst those years, I moved away from my hometown of Champaign, Illinois and settled in Denver, Colorado. I met my husband. I started a mildly extreme gym experience and after three years, sustained a mildly extreme injury. Work was suddenly intollerable and a new plan that had been simmering, had to fully cook.

I dove in to the idea of web developement. For all I knew about it, it was an easy choice. I knew it would be a huge change, but after ten years of full-contact socializing/unlicensed therapy/hair art I was ready.
