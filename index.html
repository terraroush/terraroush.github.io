Holiday Road: Lion Kings

Holiday Road: Lion Kings is a vacation itinerary building application. A user can build an itinerary comprised of a National Park, an attraction and an eatery. To begin the process, a user will select a state. That kicks off a series of events. Next the user will select a park. That list is populated via the National Park Services API. From there the user can view a photo and other helpful information in order to choose one and add it to the itinerary. Next they will choose an attraction and an eatery in the same fashion as park. These databases are provided by Nashville Software School. The app also fetches from the openweathermap.org weather API as soon as a park is choosen.

This was a group project, and our first full professional sprint. We had daily stand-ups and participated in a retrospective when it was all over. My involvement was largely in the planning and facilitating of the group's processes. I was also responsible for building the eatery section. I used the API to fetch the data and filter the data we needed.

About Me

Over the last ten years, I took over 9000 appointments as a hairdresser/stylist/guru/artist. I worked alongside and with over 40 other uniquely talented individuals. I excelled in wedding hair, detailed and picture-perfect hair color transformations and even barbering. I was always learning, trying to challenge myself, and pushing to do my best work.

Amidst those years, I moved away from my hometown of Champaign, Illinois and settled in Denver, Colorado. I met my husband. I started a mildly extreme gym experience and after three years, sustained a mildly extreme injury. Work was suddenly intollerable and a new plan that had been simmering, had to fully cook.

I dove in to the idea of web developement. For all I knew about it, it was an easy choice. I knew it would be a huge change, but after ten years of full-contact socializing/unlicensed therapy/hair art I was ready.
